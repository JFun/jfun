Privacy Policy

Rattle · Last updated July 19, 2026

Rattle is a single-player physics puzzle game. It is built to collect no data at all. There are no user accounts, no logins, no in-app purchases, no advertising, and no analytics. The app does not connect to the internet to play. This policy explains exactly what that means.

What we collect

Nothing. The app collects no personal information and no usage data. It contains no analytics SDK, no advertising SDK, and no crash-reporting service. It makes no network requests during play.

Your game progress

Your level progress and stars are saved only on your device, in the app's local storage. This data never leaves your device and is not sent to us or to anyone else. Deleting the app removes it completely.

What we never collect

  • No name, email address, phone number, or account credentials.
  • No precise or coarse location.
  • No contacts, photos, microphone, or camera access.
  • No advertising identifier (IDFA), and no ads of any kind.
  • No device identifiers, analytics identifiers, or cookies.
  • No payment or financial information.

Tracking

The app does not track you in any sense, including the sense defined by Apple's App Tracking Transparency framework. Because it collects no data, there is nothing to link, share, or sell, and the app shows no App Tracking Transparency prompt.

Third-party services

None. Rattle uses no third-party services, servers, or SDKs that collect data.

Children

Rattle is suitable for all ages. It has no chat, no social features, no purchases, and collects no information from anyone, including children.

Changes to this policy

If a future version of the app ever begins collecting data (for example, optional analytics), this page will be updated before that version ships, and the change will be reflected in the App Store privacy details.
