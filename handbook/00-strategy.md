# 00 — Strategy

## The shape that works

**A classic, instantly-graspable mechanic + exactly ONE innovation.** Gen-1 clones
with no differentiation feel flat (Block Blossom). Too many new systems kill
graspability (Underroot, Vinegrow, Thawline). The sweet spot is a mechanic players
already understand, twisted in exactly one ownable way.

## The complexity-budget law

**Hold a hard complexity budget — one mechanic can hide four systems.**
- The twist must be **felt, not computed.** If the player has to track per-entity
  state in their head (Thawline's per-piece melt numbers), it fails the
  graspability self-test. No mental bookkeeping.
- **The tutorial must be one sentence.** If you can't teach it in one line, it's
  over budget. ("Swipe to choose which way gravity pulls.")
- Vinegrow died on "3 species × growth rules × prune mode" — that's three systems
  wearing one costume. Cut until one remains.

## Gate 4 — the question you answer BEFORE greybox

> **In one sentence: what makes player N bring player N+1?**

Every concept answers this before any code. A game/app with no structural reason
to spread is a vitamin you must be *driven* to — and it goes quiet (see
[06-lessons](06-lessons.md)). This is not a marketing afterthought; it is a design
constraint at concept time. If the honest answer is "nothing," change the concept,
not the launch plan.

## Originality is also a moat

Differentiation isn't only an Apple-rejection dodge ([01](01-apple-rules.md)) — a
solver-proven original mechanic (Moraine's solve-to-par gravity) is defensible in
a way a reskinned clone never is. Do the [prior-art audit](04-prior-art-audit.md)
and be willing to **rename after research** (Gravity Shift → Moraine; the audit
worked).

## Fallback when the fun/simplicity gate fails

Don't force a failing twist. The **Vita playbook**: a pure classic
(solitaire/word-search/jigsaw) rebuilt for an underserved audience, zero new
mechanics — a known-good floor when an experimental concept doesn't clear the gate.
